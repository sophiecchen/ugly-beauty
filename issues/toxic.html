Toxic Ingredients

Unlike food, the beauty industry lacks any comprehensive regulation. However, these products still come in contact with our skin, possibly being absorbed, before being washed off into the oceans and posing a threat to marine animals and other wildlife.

Little Regulation

How is a beauty product checked for safety? In order for a product to hit the market, it only as to be checked for color additives and over-the-counter drugs. Thus, many ingredients irritate and penetrate the skin or are endocrine disruptors. The nonprofit Environmental Working Group, or EWG, found one “known or probable carcinogen” in every 120 cosmetic products on the United States market, those products ranging from lip balm to to shampoo. Because of this, thoroughly checking ingredients before buying any cosmetic product is vital for both the consumer and the environment.

Ingredients to Avoid

These are some toxic ingredients common in beauty products that should be avoided for the safety of both the consumer and the environment.

Microbeads

Even though microbeads might be banned in many western countries, the regulations are not all encompassing. Watch out for toothpaste, face washes, shampoo, eyeliner, and lip gloss that contains microbeads or plastic, possibly labeled as polypropylene or polyethylene, when shopping online.

Titanium Dioxide

Titanium dioxide, or TiO2, can be found in skin tints and mineral-based makeup. The compound can alter freshwater snail DNA and harm phytoplankton growth. Phytoplankton produces oxygen for both marine animals and humans. Titanium dioxide’s effects on the organism could severely disrupt ecosystems.

Triclosan

Triclosan is antimicrobial chemical. It disrupts hormones and irritates the skin. Alarmingly, triclosan has also been found in the Great Lakes: because it does not degrade quickly, it accumulates and causes long term issues and reacts with other chemicals to form toxins. Triclosan both reduces the lifespans and changes the biochemistry of aquatic animals.

Chemical Sunscreen

Sunscreen can either be mineral or chemical. Mineral sunscreen reflects UV rays, but it feels more sunscreen-like, which some people dislike. Chemical sunscreens, on the other hand, absorb UV light rather than reflecting it. Their light feel is more popular than mineral sunscreen.

The two most common chemical sunscreen chemicals are oxybenzone and octinoxate; both are toxic to corals. Coral reefs, which could be 90% gone by 2050, provide food, coastal protection, and habitats to marine life. Oxybenzone is especially dangerous. It disrupts coral DNA and hormones such that baby coral encase themselves and die.

These chemicals can easily travel into oceans by washing off the sunscreen in a shower or playing at the beach, and 6,000 to 14,000 metric tons of sunscreen lotions end up in coral reef areas annually.

Chemical sunscreens also can be absorbed by the skin into the bloodstream. These chemicals can disrupt hormones and are linked with cellular damage and cancer.

Parabens

Parabens are a type of preservative commonly found in cosmetics. Parabens are also partially responsible for killing off corals. They disrupt hormones in marine life, and overwhelming evidence shows that parabens from our daily lives are traveling to wildlife, building up in the tissue of bears, birds, and marine life.

Parabens are also linked with a greater risk of breast cancer since they have estrogen-mimicking properties.

Fragrance

Fragrance itself is not necessarily toxic. However, “fragrance” is a catch-all word that refers to a company’s secret formula. Chemicals toxic to humans and the environment like phthalates, which have been linked to breast cancer and reproductive birth defects, could be included without being disclosed. Fragrance has been linked to allergies and irritation as well. Look for soaps, cleaners, shampoos, and perfume without fragrances.

Check Before You Buy

Next time you buy a makeup product, you should also take a glance at the ingredients. Because cosmetics are coming in direct contact with your skin, it is especially important to make sure that these ingredients are safe and non-toxic.Toxic ingredients are far more common in makeup than you realize.

Avoid microbeads, titanium dioxide/TiO2, parabens, triclosan, chemical sunscreen, and "fragrance."
