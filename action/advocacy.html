Advocacy

Greener cosmetic choices are only a drop in the bucket. Environmentally safe products are important, but it ultimately intervenes at the wrong stage in the process. Properly addressing the environmental impacts of any industry effectively, such as the beauty industry, needs to come from top-down.

Spreading the Word

The more people are aware of makeup's environmental impacts, the greater the change people can make. Telling friends, family, or others online can also multiply your impact as they also change their makeup habits and spread the word.

Activism and Voting

By ourselves, there is only so much we can do. Recycling that product’s cardboard box does not prevent the gallons of water wasted when it was made, nor does it prevent the company from choosing poorly sourced ingredients.

Making wiser decisions is certainly important, but rallying the government and larger coorperations is what will make a difference in the long run.

We need to be activists and rally for change. Making our voices heard is what brings attention to the environmental crisis. From demonstrating and protesting to even just spreading word amongst family and friends, letting others know about the crisis is just as important as taking action individually. One person’s choices might not make much of a difference, but whole assemblies of environmentally-conscious people will.

Politically, not everyone has the power to vote. Those that do, however, can use it to hold leaders accountable and elect those that will fight for legislation in favor of the earth. Laws and legislation can regulate companies, protect consumers from deception, and prevent the earth from facing even more harm.

Nations need to step up in order to deter the environmental crisis that has already come.
