Buying Greener

With so many beauty brands in the market and rampant greenwashing, finding those that are truely clean and eco-friendly might seem difficult. Luckily, eco-friendly beauty brands are on the rise. Here are 10 brands you can check out and incorporate into your own routine.

Less is More

The most effective way to reduce your impact on the environment is to reduce the amount of products you own and buy. Most of the waste produced in a product comes from the manufacturing side, which consumers cannot control. Cutting down on the number of products you own and buy reduces the waste created to make the product and the waste created after the product is finished.

Before you buy a product, ask yourself these questions:

Do I need this makeup product? Many people have numerous red lipsticks or a colorful eyeshadow pallet of 40 colors, yet they only reach for the same lipstick, the same 4 colors in the pallet. If the whole product is not going to be used frequently, than it’s not necessary to buy. A similar question you can ask yourself is whether makeup is really necessary at all. The beauty industry capsizes on our insecurities, and high-coverage foundation or full contour is rarely necessary in everyday life.

Will I use all of the product? Keep in mind that makeup likely expires faster than you think. If you won't finish at least most of the product, then chances are that you won’t use it frequently enough to need it. For pallets, also consider whether all the colors will be used or just a few. It may be a wiser investment to buy separate colors you will definitely use.

Why am I buying this product? Are you buying the product because need it as a part of your daily makeup routine or are you buying it because it's on sale and was recommended by your favorite celebrity? There’s nothing wrong with experimenting with different products, but researching well and finishing what you have first helps reduce waste.

Research, Research, Research

Checking the ingredients, packaging, and brand boil down to taking time to research thoroughly. Information about the ethical impacts and environmental impacts of a product should not be difficult to find with even just a bit of clicking on the about section of the brand website. If it is difficult to find straightforward information about a product’s ingredients, sourcing, or manufacturing, then the brand lacks transparency.

Research on environmentally-friendly brands prevents spontaneous buying, and allows you to make well-informed decisions.

Eco-Friendly Brands

Most brands do not have their customers’ best interests in mind. Profit is the number one motivator. Therefore, don’t give your money to brands who tend to use toxic or harmful ingredients and materials. Instead, support brands that respect the environment. Choosing to hold brands accountable for the choices they make in manufacturing can give other companies incentive to make better, eco-friendly decisions. Outside of cosmetics, brand accountability can easily be applied to the clothing, personal care, etc industries.

Here are 10 cosmetic companies you can check out and to find truely green makeup to incorporate into your own routine.

1. Elate

Elate is a brand that is focused on creating effective products safe for both consumers and the environment. Their vegan and cruelty-free cosmetics are packaged in bamboo rather than plastic. The products are also refillable. The products come with a bamboo case and magnetic, stainless steel tins of pressed product. When one pressed tin is finished, it can be recycled, and another pressed tin can be snapped in its place. This saves packaging and allows consumers to choose which colors they want to buy.

2. Faraday Face

This brand makes the environment a top priority and is designed specifically toward those who are seeking “naturally effective and environmentally-conscious cosmetics.” Its ingredients are 100% non-toxic and non-GMO; in addition, they are vegan, cruelty-free, and gluten-free. Faraday Face products contain no plastic or plastic packaging. Rather they use aluminum pans and paperboard pallets, which can be recycled, and compostable ribbon. The brand also donates 5% of its profits to ocean cleanup initiatives.

3. Kjaer Weis

Kjaer Weis is a luxury Danish brand that is both well-known and eco-friendly. Like other brands on this list, Kjaer Weis is organic and not tested on animals. Kirsten Kjaer Weis, founder and makeup artist, uses her knowledge of beauty and commitment to the environment to create products that are just as high-performing, if not more, as luxury but not sustainable brands. Its stainless steel compacts are also 100% recyclable and its widely-accessible refills are packaged in compostable paper. In humid environments, stainless steel is a great sustainable alternative to bamboo, which can get moldy.

4. Axiology

Axiology is a lipstick brand offering a wide range of bold shades that calls itself “100% evil free” with the “cleanest, most ethical lipstick on the planet.” It is vegan, cruelty-free, and clean, lacking animal-testing and palm oil. The lipstick is packaged in hand-recycled cardboard and the tube is made from recycled aluminum. At the end of its life, the tube can then be recycled again. Axiology is a good way to enjoy bolder makeup while being eco-friendly.

5. RMS

Founded by Rose-Marie, RMS makeup and skincare reflects her philosophy of clean, simple, and healthy. Organic and free of toxins and metals, the brand’s Living Luminizer has a cult following. While not entirely plastic-free, RMS does include products packaged in containers of glass and stainless steel that are 100% recyclable.

6. Besame

Besame is a vintage makeup paradise. Because of this, their products are both a time capsule and plastic-free. The brand works to create historically accurate products while also maintaining “modern” values of cruelty-free, eco-friendliness and zero waste solutions in their company. Their products include classic lipstick colors and the famed cake mascara.

7. Tata Harper

Tata Harper focuses on cruelty-free and chemical-free skincare. Its high-quality ingredients are concentrated and natural, and the products are handmade in Vermont. Its packaging is also mostly recyclable with materials like paper and glass, and any plastic that is used is derived from corn rather than petroleum.

8. Lush

With accusations of greenwashing, Lush can be controversial. Despite being 100% cruelty-free, not all of its products are free of harmful synthetics. However, the brand is transparent and a leader in the industry: Lush makes “naked” products like their shampoo bars that have absolutely no packaging. They also offer packageless conditioner bars. Because the company is much more widespread and well-known than others on this list, it’s easier to find their products and return empty containers.

9. ZAO

An “eco-luxury” French brand, ZAO uses toxic-free, cruelty-free, and vegan ingredients. ZAO is also certified organic. It crafts its cosmetics with a low-carbon footprint and uses primarily sustainable bamboo packaging instead of plastic or glass. Like Elate, The brand designs its products to be refillable, which also means less packaging waste.

10. Antonym

Antonym is certified organic, vegan, and cruelty-free. The brand features eyeshadow pallets, blushes, and brushes packaged in bamboo. The outer packaging is also made with responsibly-sourced paper. Unfortunately, the containers of products like its foundation are packaged in plastic. Its products have been used at high-profile events like the AMAs and Golden Globes by Kate Mara, Elizabeth Banks, and other celebrities.
